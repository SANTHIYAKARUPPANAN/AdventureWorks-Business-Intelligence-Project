# Business Usage

This document explains how each table in the AdventureWorks database supports business analysis and decision-making.

---

## Sales Table

**Business Usage**

The Sales table records every sales transaction and serves as the primary source for revenue and order analysis. It is used to:

- Analyze total sales and revenue
- Track sales trends over time
- Measure product performance
- Evaluate customer purchasing behavior
- Compare sales across different territories

---

## Customer Table

**Business Usage**

The Customer table stores customer information and is used to:

- Analyze customer demographics
- Segment customers based on income, education, or occupation
- Identify repeat customers
- Calculate Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- Perform RFM Analysis

---

## Product Categories Table

**Business Usage**

The Product Categories table groups products into major categories. It is used to:

- Compare sales across product categories
- Identify top-performing categories
- Analyze category-wise revenue contribution

---

## Product Subcategories Table

**Business Usage**

The Product Subcategories table provides detailed classification of products. It is used to:

- Compare sales across subcategories
- Identify high-performing product segments
- Perform detailed product analysis

---

## Products Table

**Business Usage**

The Products table stores information about every product sold. It is used to:

- Identify best-selling products
- Analyze product profitability
- Compare product pricing and cost
- Evaluate product performance
- Perform inventory-related analysis

---

## Territories Table

**Business Usage**

The Territories table stores geographical information for sales analysis. It is used to:

- Compare sales by region and country
- Identify high-performing markets
- Analyze geographical sales trends
- Support regional business decisions
